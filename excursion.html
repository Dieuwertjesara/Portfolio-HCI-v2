Excursion to Nemo

For this course we had an excursion to Nemo: The Science Museum. This was a lot of fun and there where so many different things to see and more importantly, do!

Assignment

The one installation I find the most interesting in Nemo is the chain reaction show. This is not directly technological in a digital sense, but it's from a machenical perspective very interessting. The chain reaction show is done a few times every day and is a bit different every time. It starts with a big space and a whole lot of random objects placed in weird ways. At the beginning the presenter quickly does some last adjusting and checks if everything is in the exact right spot. Precission is very important. Then the show can begin. Someone from the audience is picked as an assitent and the presenter explains what the volunteer has to do. This usually involves pushing, pulling or pressing an object. This sets the chain reaction in motion. One smalle change in the chain sets the next thing off, and the next one and so on. It starts small but becomes bigger and bigger as it goes on. One of the staples in the show is a big deskchair (the red thing behind my head in the picture). This will at some point twirl all the way down and it always seems as if it's gonna crash into the ground. This ofcourse doesn't happen because the show is very well thought out.

The show ends with a rocket launched into the sky severel floors up.

the chain reaction show teaches kids about action and reaction and how a small thing can set other things in motion. This is in essence like an object that responds to what you programmed it to respond to in a way you pre defined. That is also action -> reaction. One little thing out of place or missing can stop the entire process.

This makes it so interesting to me. It's a very basic hands on example of many things we later in life do. By understanding the basics you can make more complicated projects. It also shows it doens't matter how weird or out of the box you make something as long as the basic principle is kept in mind.
They could've simply explained it with 10 domino's that fell because of the first, but by making it this big and crazy and by adding a ton of elements that behave out of the orinary you don't develop this invisible ceiling.

This picture was just for fun. It was also very impressive but less than the chain reaction.
